Province Profile

Situated at the southernmost tip of China, Hainan overlooks Guangdong across the Qiongzhou Strait to the north, Vietnam across the Beibu Bay to the west. Its neighbors in the South China Sea include the Philippines, Brunei and Malaysia. The jurisdiction of Hainan covers Hainan Island, the Xisha (Paracel) Islands, the Zhongsha (Macclesfield) Islands and the Nansha (Spratly) Islands as well as the surrounding maritime areas.

Economic Development

In 2014, the province realized GDP of 1,768.99 billion yuan, up 9.7 percent year on year. In a breakdown, the primary industry achieved 156.49 billion yuan, up 5.1 percent year on year; the secondary industry achieved 968.98 billion yuan, up 11.2 percent year on year; and the tertiary industry achieved 643.52 billion yuan, up 8.4 percent year on year. The industrial added value of enterprises each with over 20 million yuan of annual business revenue reached 746.86 billion yuan in the year, up 11.3 percent year on year.
